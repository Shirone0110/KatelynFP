Sources


The sources I've complied are a mixture between very specific and very broad webpages. Some of them talk about anxiety as a whole and present statistics about anxiety and some talk about anxiety in certain groups of people.


  • The Anxiety and Depression Association of America is a website that gives a good background on anxiety and is used around the world. It gives many specific statistics about anxiety as a whole and provides the public with useful information as well. This website is credible because it is a .org and it is nationally used.
    Anxiety and Depression Association of America

  • This source gives information on anxiety in pregnant women. It gives specific stats, talks about the symptoms of anxiety in pregnant women, and talks about the causes of the anxiety. This is a credible website because it is a .org and is ran by healthcare workers.
    Anxiety in Pregnant Women

  • This source gives information on college student athletes with anxiety. It gives stats on college student athletes and has interviews as well. This source is credible because it is a edu and it is a research website.
    Anxiety in College Student Athletes

  • This source talks about anxiety in regular student athletes. It gives statistics regarding student athletes and talks about the causes and symptoms of the anxiety they have. This source is credible because it is a .org and it is a part of a government ran website(NCAA)
    Anxiety in Student Athletes

  • This source talks about anxiety in teen girls. It gives statictics about teen anxiety and talks about the causes and symptoms of the anxiety they experience. This source is credible because it is a .org and is a research website.
    Anxiety in Teen Girls
